# Signal-to-Message Playbook

## Signal 1: The Product Launch (Agentic Commerce Example)
- **Research**: Find a new feature launch in their blog/LinkedIn.
- **The Message Logic**: Validate the launch -> Identify the technical debt/challenge it likely created -> Offer a peer solution.
- **Example**: "Congrats on the launch of [X]. Now that [X] is live, I'm curious how your team is approaching the challenge of [Technical Consequence, e.g., data synchronization]..."

## Signal 2: The Lookalike Customer (Coinbase/Stripe Example)
- **Research**: Identify a Cursor customer in a similar vertical.
- **The Message Logic**: Mention the specific technical project the lookalike is doing with Cursor.
- **Example**: "We're supporting Coinbase as they navigate a similar protocol divide. They moved away from fine-tuning to leveraging extended context windows..."

## Signal 3: The Job Posting (The "Onboarding" Hook)
- **Research**: Find a Senior Dev job post.
- **The Message Logic**: Reference the tech stack mentioned -> Focus on "Time-to-first-PR" or "Onboarding velocity."
- **Example**: "I noticed you're scaling your [React/Rust] team. Usually, that comes with a massive cognitive load for new hires. Cursor helps speed up that 'Aha!' moment by indexing the codebase semantically..."

## Signal 4: The Personal Hook (Salt & Straw/Yanks Example)
- **Research**: LinkedIn "About" or recent posts.
- **The Message Logic**: Proof of human intent.
- **Constraint**: Keep it light and separate from the business value.
